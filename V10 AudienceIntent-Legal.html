3. Free Tools — Report Generator & Lost Revenue Calculator

These tools are provided free of charge for informational and indicative purposes only. Results generated are AI-assisted estimates based on publicly available data and user-provided information. They do not constitute a formal business audit, professional assessment, or guarantee of any specific outcome.

Free Business Performance Report Generator (report.audienceintent.ai)

When you use the Free Business Performance Report Generator, you provide us with your business name, industry, location, website URL and Google Business Profile URL. We use this information to:

  • Query the Google Places API (New) for your business's publicly available GBP data
  • Query SerpAPI to retrieve publicly available Google Maps data including recent reviews, posts and related businesses
  • Scrape your public website for technical signals (live status, chat widget presence, SMS opt-in indicators)
  • Pass this data to the Anthropic Claude API to generate a written performance analysis

The report is generated in real time and displayed in your browser. We do not store the full report output on our servers. However, your submitted business details (name, URL, location) may be retained in our systems for service improvement and follow-up communication purposes.

Data sources used in this tool: Google Places API (New), SerpAPI Google Maps, Anthropic Claude AI, public website scraping. All data sources access only publicly available information.

Lost Revenue Calculator (lostrevenue.audienceintent.ai)

The Lost Revenue Calculator collects your responses to a short questionnaire about your business (number of monthly leads, average job value, close rate, and missed call/response rate). This data is used solely to calculate an estimated revenue gap figure. We do not store individual calculator responses linked to personal identifiers unless you subsequently provide your contact details.

Important: Revenue figures, scores and estimates produced by both tools are illustrative only. They are based on general industry assumptions and publicly available data signals. They should not be relied upon for business, financial or investment decisions without independent verification.
