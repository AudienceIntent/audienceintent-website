## Why Local AEO Is a Different Problem Entirely

Most AEO content focuses on one thing: structuring long-form articles so AI engines extract them as answers. That works well for informational queries. "What is a 1031 exchange?" or "How does HVAC zoning work?" are questions where a well-structured article can earn a citation.

Local queries work differently. When someone asks ChatGPT "best HVAC company near me" or "top-rated dentist in Austin," the AI isn't looking for your blog. It's evaluating:

* **Google Business Profile completeness** - categories, services, hours, photos, Q&A
* **Review recency and volume** - [98% of consumers check reviews before visiting a local business](https://www.forbes.com/advisor/business/software/online-review-statistics/)
* **Structured citations** across directories and data aggregators
* **Entity authority** - how consistently your business name, address, and phone appear across the web
* **Conversion signals** - response time, booking availability, website speed

### The SaaS Agency Mismatch

The agencies dominating AEO roundups built their reputations on content-led growth for software companies. Their playbooks are legitimate for that audience. But when a franchise marketer hires them expecting AI visibility across 30 locations, they get a content strategy and a reporting dashboard - not optimized GBP listings, not a review generation system, not call handling that prevents missed leads from becoming lost revenue.

**This is the gap.** Local AEO isn't primarily a content problem. It's an operations and infrastructure problem. The AI has to trust your business before it recommends it. That trust is built through consistent signals across dozens of touchpoints, most of which have nothing to do with blog posts.

> "Local AEO optimizes for extraction into AI overviews, voice search, and single-answer responses." - Nova Brandworks

The implication for multi-location operators: you need a vendor that understands local search infrastructure, not just content production. Those are two very different service categories, and most roundups don't distinguish between them.

## What to Actually Look For in a Local AEO Agency

Before reviewing any shortlist, franchise marketers need a clear evaluation framework. The criteria that matter for local businesses are fundamentally different from what SaaS buyers should care about.

### Why Speed-to-Lead Belongs in This Conversation

Most AEO discussions stop at visibility. But for local businesses, visibility without conversion infrastructure is wasted. [The first business to respond wins 78% of leads](https://audienceintent.ai/insights), and [85% of missed callers never call back](https://audienceintent.ai). An agency that gets you cited by ChatGPT but leaves your phones unattended after 5pm has solved half the problem.

**For multi-location operators, this compounds.** A franchise with 20 locations that misses 10 calls per location per week is losing 200 potential customers every week, regardless of AI visibility scores.

### Questions to Ask Every Agency

Before signing anything, ask these directly:

1. How do you track AI citations specifically for local queries, not just branded mentions?
2. Can you show per-location reporting, or is everything aggregated?
3. What's your process for keeping GBP listings current across all locations?
4. Do you handle lead response, or is that the client's responsibility?
5. What's your timeline from onboarding to measurable AI visibility improvement?

Agencies that hesitate on questions 1, 2, or 3 are not built for multi-location local work.

## What Most "Best AEO Agencies" Roundups Get Wrong

The existing roundups ranking AEO agencies share a consistent set of blind spots that make them actively misleading for local business buyers.

### They Rank Agencies on Content Metrics, Not Local Signals

The most common evaluation criteria in published roundups are things like "thought leadership quality," "content depth," and "AI citation rate for informational queries." These matter for SaaS companies trying to rank for industry terms. They are almost irrelevant for a plumber trying to appear in "best emergency plumber near me" results.

### They Confuse GEO with Local AEO

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) and local AEO overlap but are not the same thing. GEO focuses on getting brand mentions and citations in AI-generated answers to broad queries. Local AEO focuses specifically on appearing as a recommended business for location-specific, service-specific queries. Most roundups use the terms interchangeably, which leads buyers toward agencies that solve the wrong problem.

### They Ignore the Conversion Gap

**Getting cited by an AI engine means nothing if the lead can't reach you.** Research shows that [AI traffic converts at 14.2%, compared to Google's 2.8%](https://www.wearetenet.com/) - but only when the business actually captures the lead. An agency that optimizes your AI visibility without addressing call handling, response time, and booking infrastructure is leaving the most valuable part of the equation unsolved.

### They Don't Disclose Methodology

Common red flags across published agency roundups include self-ranking without disclosed methods, vague AEO methodologies, and conflicting service claims. If an agency appears in its own "best of" list without explaining how they ranked themselves, treat it as a marketing exercise, not an evaluation.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is AEO and how is it different from SEO for local businesses?

Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) is the practice of structuring your business's online presence so that AI engines like ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Perplexity, and Siri recommend you as a direct answer to queries. For local businesses, this goes beyond content. It includes Google Business Profile optimization, review generation, structured citations, entity consistency, and conversion infrastructure. Traditional SEO focuses on ranking in search results. Local AEO focuses on becoming the recommended answer before a search result is ever clicked.

### How much does a local AEO agency typically cost?

Pricing varies widely. Content-focused AEO agencies typically charge $2,000 to $8,000 per month for strategy and content production. Full-service local AEO systems that include GBP management, review automation, lead handling, and AI visibility work start around $997 per month for single-market operators. Multi-location and franchise pricing depends on the number of locations and scope of managed services. Always ask for per-location pricing and what's included in execution versus what requires your team's involvement.

### How long does it take to see results from local AEO?

For GBP optimization and structured citation work, measurable improvements in local pack and AI citation frequency typically appear within 60 to 90 days. Review velocity improvements can show results faster, often within 30 days of implementing an automated review system. AI citation tracking is still an emerging reporting category - expect agencies to use proprietary monitoring tools rather than standardized metrics.

### Do I need AEO if I already do local SEO?

Yes. Local SEO and local AEO address different surfaces. SEO targets ranked results in Google. AEO targets the AI-generated answers that now appear above, beside, or instead of those results. With AI Overviews appearing in 68% of local searches and zero-click rates between 75% and 82%, a business that only does local SEO is optimizing for a shrinking share of the available visibility.

### What should a franchise marketer prioritize first?

Start with Google Business Profile completeness and review velocity across all locations. These are the two highest-leverage inputs for AI citation in local queries and the fastest to show measurable results. Once those foundations are solid, layer in structured citations, schema markup, and lead capture automation. AI visibility without conversion infrastructure is an incomplete solution.
