## How AI Search Works Differently – And What It Demands from Your Business

Google looks for keywords and backlinks. AI looks for entities, facts, and relationships. It wants to know *who* you are and *why* you can be trusted to solve a specific problem.

To get picked, you must feed the AI exactly what it needs:

- **Authoritative Content:** AI draws on well-structured web pages including service pages, FAQ sections, and blog posts.

- **Accurate Profiles:** It requires a complete Google Business Profile with accurate hours, service areas, and categories.

- **Real Engagement:** Signals like calls, direction requests, and photo views now carry more weight in rankings.

### AI Signals vs. Google Algorithms

Getting ranked by AI is significantly harder than traditional SEO. You can't just buy links or stuff keywords. The barrier to entry is higher because the scrutiny is deeper.

In fact, securing AI visibility is **3 to 30 times harder** than achieving Google local rankings (Search Engine Land). This difficulty is actually good news for high-quality businesses. It filters out the spam and leaves room for legitimate operators to dominate.

### Local Visibility: Structured Data Wins

The language of AI is code, specifically **Schema markup**. If your website is just text, AI has to guess what you do. If you use structured data, you are handing the AI a business card it can read instantly.

Structured data via **JSON-LD markup** is essential as AI relies on it for accurate representation (Ariel Digital Marketing). Without it, you are hoping for luck. With it, you are ensuring clarity.
